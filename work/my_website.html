the title

I write music reviews on a website called Album of the Year , on my profile, pixelcouch . I have more recently tended towards soundtrack reviews because that's what I listen to, being someone who doesn't really sit down and listen to new music often, but I do have a decent, if small, collection of actual album reviews - the best ones being my CLariS reviews, as they're my favourite artists, but really I think any review I have written since Soen's 'Tellurian' is worth reading.

My favourite reviews I have written do not necessarily correspond to my favourite albums, as you can see. The list is in no particular order since I have no clue which is my actual top favourite.

Weezer - SZNZ: Winter

This one is a fairly early review, from back in the good old days when I actually wrote them within a reasonable timeframe. Still, I think it reflects my current style nicely, going song-by-song and then ending with overall thoughts, analysis and closing remarks. This was mostly chosen due to my analysis of the SZNZ project as a whole, something I'm quite proud of, as it justified my love for a set of albums which most people agree are pretty bad.
Shinkichi Mitsumune - Rozen Maiden Original Soundtrack

This one, on the other hand, is much more recent - one of the newest reviews I've written, and for one of my favurite soundtracks to one of my favourite shows of all time. I believe this is the longest review on my page currently (though an even longer one is in the works and nearing completion), despite me intentionally skipping sixteen songs, which says a lot about how much I love this soundtrack.
CLariS - Fairy Party

And the third and final notable review is actually one I gave a mixed rating - however, I think this is my best and most comprehensive CLariS review to date; even the uninteresting 'J-Pop slop' as I referred to it got fairly lengthy commentary and description, with compliments given to good bits and explanations given for why I didn't like them overall. Then there's the much more involved commentary on the songs I did like, which again is the most comprehensive of maybe any review to date, with 'Parallel Warp', 'Last Squall', 'TRAVEL', and 'Cynical Suspense' all getting lengthy paragraphs with a lot of detail.
